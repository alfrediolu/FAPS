Help Center


The help section provides information of how to use FAPS website and API. There are two options for input.

  • Single-Protein Lookup
  • CSV-Based Search

In the Single-Protein Lookup, you can search with your protein of interest with an accession ID (e.g. "P08670", "Q9Y490"). You can also upload a .csv file with a column name 'Accession' in the CSV-Based Search option. The output will demonstrate the simulated 3-D structure of proteins and some experimentally determined secondary structure information, which includes helix percentage, known partial helix percentage, beta strand percentage, known partial beta strand percentage, turn/random coil percentage, and known partial turn percentage for each protein.



Results

The result summarizes a set of secondary structure information of the protein of interest in a table. First column of the table indicates the accession ID/ protein ID that was searched for and the second column provides information about the simulation type. Next three columns (Sim Alpha, Sim Beta, Sim Turn) give information about the 3-D structure information that was experimentally determined from SWISS-PROT database. The next nine columns (Uniprot Alpha, Uniprot Beta, Uniprot Turn, Known Proportion, Unknown Proportion, Sequence Length, Overall Alpha, Overall Beta, Overall Turn) represent secondary structure information that was computed from UniProt database for a given accesion ID.

Each of the structure’s proportion (alpha, beta and turn) was calculated by dividing their total length by the sum of all parameter’s length. For instance, the total length of alpha helix structure of P08670 was 194 and the sum of all structures’ (alpha helix, beta strand and turn) length was 203. Therefore, the alpha percentage of the accession code P08670 was calculated (194/203)*100 or 95.5665% (Uniprot Alpha). The known proportion and unknown proportion are the total percentages of known structure vs total protein sequence length. So the known proportion is the sum of the 3 secondary structures and the unknown is just the known proportion subtracted from 1.
